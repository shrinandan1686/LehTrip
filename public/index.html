Paperwork
PERMITS & PERMISSIONS
✅ JUST PAY ONLINE
Ladakh Environment Fee (EDF)
Indian tourists no longer need a separate Inner Line Permit. Just pay ₹400/person Environment Fee at lahdclehpermit.in. Valid for Nubra, Pangong, Tso Moriri, Khardung La. Print and carry receipt ×4 copies per person.
✅ NOT REQUIRED
Leh City Sightseeing
No permit needed for Leh palace, Shanti Stupa, markets, monasteries in and around Leh town, or travel on Srinagar-Leh and Manali-Leh highways.
⚠️ EDF RECEIPT NEEDED
Nubra Valley + Khardung La
Show EDF payment receipt at checkpost. Carry Aadhar + printed EDF receipt. Apply and print before leaving Bangalore.
⚠️ EDF RECEIPT NEEDED
Pangong Tso Lake
Show EDF at Chang La checkpost. Military is strict here — have all 4 persons' receipts and Aadhar cards ready in one folder before approaching checkpost.
⚠️ ARMY ARRANGED
Turtuk → Thang Village
Thang is India's northernmost village, 2.5 km from the Pakistan LOC. No online permit — your Turtuk homestay owner arranges army permission the evening before. Tell them on check-in. At the Thang checkpost: surrender original Aadhar card on entry, collect on exit. All 4 persons required to be present. Indians only.
⚠️ EDF RECEIPT NEEDED
Hanle + Umling La (5,798m)
EDF receipt is sufficient for Hanle and the Umling La road. No additional permit needed. Show EDF at the Hanle checkpost. Carry all 4 Aadhar cards. The Hanle→Umling La route via Photi La does not require a special pass beyond EDF for Indian citizens.
⚠️ EDF RECEIPT NEEDED
Tsomoriri (Tso Moriri)
Show EDF at Upshi checkpost before turning south toward Tsomoriri. Same EDF receipt covers this. Have all 4 receipts and Aadhar in one folder. Checkpost is at Upshi junction — keep documents accessible before you reach it.
✅ NOT REQUIRED
Jammu & Kashmir Travel
No permits needed for Indian citizens to travel within J&K (Jammu, Srinagar, Sonmarg, Kargil). Just carry standard ID proof (Aadhar). Vehicle RC and insurance at checkposts.
💡 RECOMMENDED
Travel Insurance
Not mandatory but strongly recommended for high-altitude mountain travel. Cover all 4 people. Must include emergency helicopter evacuation coverage for Ladakh region. ₹500–800/person.
Expert Guidance
IMPORTANT TIPS
⛰️ Altitude Sickness (AMS)
  • Take Diamox from Day 13 as prescribed by doctor
  • Drink 3–4 litres water/day at altitude (not less)
  • Absolutely no alcohol above 3,000m
  • Light meals only — heavy food worsens symptoms
  • Rest 24 hrs on first arrival in Leh. No exertion.
  • Carry pulse oximeter — SpO2 below 80% = descend
  • Severe headache + vomiting + loss of balance = DESCEND NOW
  • Garlic soup is a local remedy for mild headaches
🌿 Vegetarian Food Guide
  • Srinagar: Krishna Vaishno Bhojnalaya (Dal Gate), Pincer Vegetarian Foods
  • Sonmarg: Hotel Sonamarg Palace — pure veg restaurant
  • Kargil: Hotel Caravan veg eatery. Carry packed food from Sonmarg.
  • Mulbekh: Small dhaba opposite Buddha statue — veg only
  • Khalsi: Samyas Restaurant — Jain cuisine, pure veg
  • Leh: Tibetan Kitchen, Bon Appetit, Gesmo, Wonderland
  • Manali: Excellent pure veg + South Indian options
  • Always carry MTR pouches + Maggi for emergencies
🗣️ Language & Safety
  • Chandigarh, Jammu: English widely spoken at hotels
  • Srinagar: Tourist areas are very English-friendly
  • Kargil, Leh: Hotel staff understand basic English
  • Learn: "Kitna hua?" (How much?) "Shudh shakahari?" (Pure veg?)
  • Download Google Translate offline: Hindi + Kashmiri
  • Book hotels on booking.com/MakeMyTrip only
  • Use Ola/Rapido in cities — metered, no scam
  • Kashmiri people are genuinely warm to South Indian tourists
🚙 Thar Roxx Mountain Driving
  • Engage 4H (4WD) on Zojila, Rohtang, rough patches
  • Engage 4L (low range) on extremely steep/icy sections
  • Engine brake on descents — don't ride the brake pedal
  • Keep 150km fuel range at all times in Ladakh
  • Fill up completely at Leh before Manali direction
  • At passes: stop briefly, don't idle engine long
  • Never drive after dark in mountain areas
  • BRO helpline: 1800-180-3474 for road conditions
🛡️ Safety & Scam Prevention
  • Never exchange currency with non-bank people
  • ATMs: use machines inside petrol stations or bank branches
  • Pre-book hotels — don't book at highway touts
  • At checkposts: be polite, have documents ready, don't argue
  • Don't photograph military installations, checkposts, soldiers
  • Carry cash — many Ladakh areas have no card machines
  • Keep ₹5,000 emergency cash per person always
  • Share real-time location with family back home
💻 WFH Tips — Manali (June 3–11)
  • Best WiFi areas: Old Manali (Manu Temple road), Mall Road hotels, Vashisht village
  • Jio signal: Good in Manali town, patchy in Vashisht, very weak in Solang Valley
  • BSNL: More reliable for voice calls in remote pockets — carry both SIMs
  • Confirm upload speed before booking — minimum 10 Mbps for video calls
  • Schedule video calls in the morning (9–12 PM) — afternoons get foggy and connectivity dips
  • Cafes with reliable WiFi: Dylan's Toasted & Roasted, Cafe 1947 (Old Manali)
  • Power cuts are common in Manali evenings — carry a charged power bank to every call
  • Temperature in early June: 18–25°C days, 10°C nights — perfect for focused work
💳 Money & Finance
  • Use Splitwise app — create group from Day 1
  • Set: Fuel 50-50, Stay 50-50 (2 rooms), Food 50-50
  • Settle daily — don't let it accumulate
  • Carry ₹20,000 cash per couple for remote areas
  • UPI works in Leh town and Manali but not at passes
  • Chandigarh has all ATMs — withdraw before heading north
  • Kargil has ATMs — last reliable withdrawal before Leh
  • Fuel bill — save all receipts for exact fuel calculation
⛽ Fuel Stops — Ladakh Critical Map
  • Rule: Never leave Leh with less than a full tank. Carry 10L jerry can always.
  • Leh city — Multiple pumps. Fill before EVERY excursion.
  • Kargil — Last reliable pump before Leh on inbound route
  • Diskit (Nubra) — Small pump, usually available. Confirm before leaving Leh.
  • Panamik — No pump. Rely on Leh fill + jerry can.
  • Turtuk / Thang — No pump. Leh → Turtuk = 205km. Full tank + jerry = comfortable.
  • Chumathang — No pump. Fill in Leh before Tsomoriri run.
  • Tsomoriri / Korzok — No pump.
  • Hanle — No pump. Leh → Hanle = 255km. Full tank + jerry = safe.
  • Umling La — No pump. Fill in Leh, fill in Hanle if possible (jerry can top-up).
  • Pang — No pump. Changthang road has nothing.
  • Keylong — Last pump before Manali on return. Always fill here.
  • Manali — Multiple pumps. Fill before CHD run.
